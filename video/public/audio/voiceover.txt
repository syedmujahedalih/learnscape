Textbooks can tell students what happens. But a student can memorize the formula and still keep the wrong model of what causes it.

Learnscape turns course material into an interactive world. It identifies the relationship worth understanding, a misconception worth testing, and the experience that can create evidence.

Before touching the controls, the learner makes a prediction and states their confidence. A wrong answer is not failure. It is useful evidence about how they currently think.

Learnscape uses that evidence to prepare one clean experiment. Here, it changes only the bob's mass while keeping length and release angle fixed.

The student releases the pendulum. The validated physics reference and a learned next-state forecast make the hidden dynamics visible.

Now the original belief meets the observation. Both bobs have the same ideal period. Mass changes energy, not swing time.

The learner must explain the result and transfer the rule to a new situation. Progress is earned through transfer, not clicking through content.

Learnscape combines a domain model, interactive world, learner model, and adaptive tutor. Codex and GPT-5.6 Terra helped turn that architecture into a tested prototype.
